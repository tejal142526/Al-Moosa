What is In-Vitro
Fertilisation (IVF)?

In-Vitro Fertilisation (IVF) is one of the most
advanced and effective fertility treatments available.
It involves fertilising an egg with sperm outside the
body in a laboratory setting and then transferring
the resulting embryo(s) into the uterus. IVF is often
recommended for couples facing various fertility
challenges, including blocked fallopian tubes, male
infertility, or unexplained infertility.

Who Can Benefit from IVF?
IVF may be the right choice for you if:

  • Women suffering from endometriosis, ovulation disorders or premature ovarian failure

  • Women considering fertility preservation (e.g. due to cancer treatment or family planning preferences)

  • Couples where the male partner has male factor infertility (low sperm count, poor motility, or abnormal morphology)

  • Couples with unexplained infertility after other treatments have failed

  • Women with blocked or damaged fallopian tubes

Is IVF painful?

While the IVF process involves some discomfort, most patients find it manageable. Egg retrieval is performed under sedation.

How long does the IVF process take?

The entire IVF process usually takes 10 to 14 days. However, the timeline may vary depending on your individual treatment plan.

How many IVF cycles should I try?

The number of recommended cycles varies, but many couples undergo 1-3 IVF cycles before achieving success. Our specialists will guide you based on your situation.

What are the success rates of IVF?

Success rates vary depending on factors like age, underlying fertility issues, and overall health.

Are there any risks associated with IVF?

Like any medical procedure, IVF carries some risks, such as ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome (OHSS) or multiple pregnancies. Our team will closely monitor you to minimise these risks.
