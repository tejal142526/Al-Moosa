Frequently Asked Questions

Is Ovulation induction painful?

Ovulation induction is a non-invasive treatment. The medications are typically taken orally or via injections, and any discomfort is minimal.

How long does ovulation induction take?

Ovulation induction usually involves taking oral medications for five days at the start of your cycle. You may have two more scans during the cycle to check your response and if the medication has worked.

What are the success rates of ovulation induction?

Success rates vary depending on factors like age, underlying fertility issues, and overall health. 50% of women who have ovulation induction get pregnant with 6 treatment cycles. Our team will provide you with realistic expectations during your consultation.

Is IVF painful?

While the IVF process involves some discomfort, most patients find it manageable. Egg retrieval is performed under sedation.

How long does the IVF process take?

The entire IVF process usually takes 10 to 14 days. However, the timeline may vary depending on your individual treatment plan.

What are the success rates of IVF?

Success rates vary depending on factors like age, underlying fertility issues, and overall health.

How many IVF cycles should I try?

The number of recommended cycles varies, but many couples undergo 1-3 IVF cycles before achieving success. Our specialists will guide you based on your situation.

Are there any risks associated with IVF?

Like any medical procedure, IVF carries some risks, such as ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome (OHSS) or multiple pregnancies. Our team will closely monitor you to minimise these risks.

Is PGT safe for embryos?

Yes, PGT is a safe and well-established procedure. The biopsy process is carefully performed by our expert embryologists. Our embryologists will be happy to speak to you about the procedure.

Who should consider PGT?

PGT is recommended for couples with a history of genetic disorders, recurrent miscarriages, or failed IVF cycles, as well as women of advanced maternal age.

Is IUI painful?

The IUI procedure is well tolerated with minimal discomfort similar to a Pap smear.

How long does the IUI process take?

The entire process, from consultation to the procedure, usually takes one menstrual cycle. However, the actual IUI procedure takes between 5 to 10 minutes.

What are the success rates of IUI?

Success rates vary depending on factors like age, underlying fertility issues, and overall health.

How many IUI cycles should I try before considering other treatments?

The number of recommended cycles varies, but many couples try between 3-6 IUI cycles before exploring other options like IVF. Our specialists will guide you based on your situation.

How successful is Frozen Embryo Transfer (FET)?

FET success rates are comparable to, and sometimes higher than, fresh embryo transfers, thanks to advances in freezing and thawing techniques.

Is FET safer than fresh embryo transfer?

FET is considered safe and may reduce the risk of complications like ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome (OHSS) associated with fresh cycles.

How long can embryos remain frozen?

Embryos can be safely stored for up to 10 years without losing their viability. There is no significant decline in quality over time.

What is the difference between a natural and medicated FET cycle?

A natural cycle relies on your natural ovulation, while a medicated cycle uses hormones that you are given to prepare the uterine lining. Your doctor will recommend the best option for you.

Does FET require anaesthesia?

The embryo transfer process is quick and well tolerated requiring no anaesthesia.

How long does the PGT process take?

The entire process, including IVF and genetic testing, typically takes between 3- 4 weeks.
